How to Apply Acrylic Nails

Looking to spice up your fingernails? Plain polish not doing it for you? With a bit of practice, you can create your own salon-worthy acrylic nails!

Supplies Needed

  • nail file
  • primer
  • acrylic liquid monomer
  • acrylic polymer powder
  • brush
  • towel

Optional Supplies

  • glitter
  • polish
  • gel topcoat
  • nail forms or glue-on tips
  • nail drill

Instructions

Watch this video by a Naio Nails expert to learn the basics of using acrylic monomer liquid and polymer powder and learn about bead sizes.
Step 1 Clean and lightly file the shiny top layer of the nail.
Step 2 Dip the brush in the liquid monomer and take a medium bead of polymer powder.
Step 3 Place the acrylic bead on the center of the nail bed. Gently pat and smooth into place.
Step 4 Dip the brush in monomer and wipe off on the towel to keep the brush clean.
Step 5 Take another medium-sized bead and form the edge of the nail, blending with the first bead.
Step 6 Using a wetter bead, place it on the top of the nail, 1/16th of an inch from the cuticle.
Step 7 Gently smooth out the wet acrylic, blending the nail.
Step 8 Allow the acrylic to fully cure and dry.
Step 9 Using either a file or a nail drill, trim the edges and smooth out the hard acrylic.
Step 10 Buff the nail and apply topcoat or polish if desired.

Learning to do acrylic nails takes a lot of time, practice, and dedication. Give yourself enough time to get the hang of how the monomer and polymer powder interact.
